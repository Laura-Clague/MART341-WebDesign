Both IP address and the DNS act as maps to different 'locations' on the internet. They both work in collaboration with each other. An IP address identifies any given computer/device and the DNS converts a series of letters (web address) to a series of numbers associated with IP address.
A cookie is a bit of information saved by your web browser. Once you visit a website, those bits of information (the cookie) are recorded and used to recognize your device later on. Often they keep track of your activity on that website.
Protocol, domain and path. Protocol states how a browser will communicate with a server. Domain is an string of identification on the internet. Path are instructions on how to navigate directories.
